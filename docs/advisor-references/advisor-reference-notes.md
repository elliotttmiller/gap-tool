Liability/Lawsuit

Umbrella policies don’t come in $500K amounts, they only come in $1M blocks.  So we probably need to have this list some of the numbers differently
We have to be careful with using words like “Recommended Umbrella” since this is technically an illustrative calculator (and not a recommendation tool). The Umbrella Needed number is fine because it shows a gap (but the description should suggest Umbrella’s come in $1M blocks)
The calculation for Wage Garnishment Risk should be 25% of “disposable income” (which essentially is gross income minus FICA and taxes); not sure how we show this as a “disclosure”, but I think right now we’re a touch too high on our calculation because that isn’t factored in.
Example, someone making $300K would have “disposable earnings” of roughly $195K.  If they are 41 today and get 3% increases in income each year, their total wage garnishment exposure would be $1,678,290. 
For optional extended asset inputs, we probably just need Home Equity (versus home value and mortgage balance), and I’m not sure what Lawsuit Exposure is for but can probably be removed.
Unemployment

Generally, we recommend they save 3-6 months of their household expenses (with 3 months being the minimum, 6 months being ideal, and anything over that should be moved towards higher yielding accounts.  The gauge between 3 and 6 months would depend based on the incomes of a spouse though.
i.e. if someone spends $20,000 a month, they should have $60K-$120K in savings.  But they can be closer to 3 months if both people in the home make $300K each (probably seeing a net income of $195K per person).  The odds of both incomes going away are small, so we may want to think of some calculation that accounts for this.
i.e. income of $300K and $100K would suggest that if the $300K goes away, roughly $65K of net income ($5,417) is still coming in every month.  So if expenses are $20K a month, they would bleed $14,583 from savings. That means remaining income ($5,417) covers 27% of monthly expenses. So I’m wondering if we suggest the following:
If highest income goes away, when remaining income covers:
Less than 33% of monthly expenses, ideal savings is 6 months
Less than 50% but more than 33%, ideal savings is 5 months
Less than 67% but more than 50%, ideal savings is 4 months
Greater than 67%, ideal savings is 3 months.
I did the math, at each of those percentages they would account for 2, 3, and 4 months’ worth of expenses respectively. So those seemed logical to me.
