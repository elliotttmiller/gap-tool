1. Life Insurance Module

1.1 Core Gap Calculator

Years to Retirement

yearsToRetirement = retirementAge - currentAge

The number of years from the client's current age to their target retirement age. All income and coverage projections are computed over this window.

Planning Horizon

Annual Client Need

annualClientNeed = annualIncome × incomeReplacementRatio

Scales the client's gross annual income by the desired income-replacement ratio to produce the yearly dollar amount the survivor family would need.

Metric Card

Annual Replacement Need

annualReplacementNeed = max(0, annualClientNeed - spouseAnnualIncome)

Offsets the annual client need by the surviving spouse's independent income to prevent double-counting.

Metric Card

Future Income Lost (PV Mode)

futureIncomeLost = Σ_{year=1}^{replacementYears} annualReplacementNeed × (1 + incomeGrowthRate)^year ───────────────────────────────────────────────── (1 + discountRate)^year

Each future year's replacement need is grown by income growth rate and discounted back to today's dollars. Summing yields a lump-sum equivalent.

PV AssumptionMetric Card

Future Income Lost (Simple Mode)

futureIncomeLost = annualReplacementNeed × replacementYears

When present-value discounting is disabled, multiplies constant annual replacement need by the number of replacement years.

Simple Mode

Base Protection Need

baseProtectionNeed = futureIncomeLost + debtsTotal + educationGoal + finalExpenses

Adds outstanding debts, education funding goals, and final expenses to the income-replacement component.

Total Death Benefit Need

Available Resources & Gap

availableResources = existingCoverageTotal + liquidAssetOffset remainingGap = max(0, baseProtectionNeed - availableResources) coverageOffsetPercentage = availableResources ÷ baseProtectionNeed

Available resources include existing coverage plus optional liquid assets. The gap is the shortfall; offset percentage shows the funded fraction.

Headline Metrics

Annuity Payment (Income Yield)

annuityPayment = PV × r / (1 - (1+r)^(-n)) If r = 0: annuityPayment = PV / n

Converts a lump-sum death benefit into the equivalent level annual income stream over the coverage period.

Income Yield

Survivor Gap & Cumulative Metrics

survivorGap[year] = max(0, projectedIncome[year] - gliCovered[year] - privateCovered[year]) cumulativeSurvivorGap = Σ survivorGap[year] lifetimeIncomeUncoveredPercentage = cumulativeSurvivorGap ÷ projectedIncomeToRetirement

Calculates annual and cumulative uncovered income, plus the percentage of total lifetime income left unprotected.

1.2 Income Gap Scenarios

Funding Base & Net Need

coverageFundingBase = groupLifeCoverage + privateLifeCoverage + nonQualifiedAssets annualNetIncomeNeed = max(0, annualIncome × incomeReplacementRatio - spouseAnnualIncome)

The investable pool available to the survivor. Life insurance death benefits are tax-free, so no gross-to-net conversion is applied.

Module 1 — Safe Withdrawal Rate

annualSafeWD = coverageFundingBase × r / (1 - (1+r)^(-years)) If r = 0: annualSafeWD = fundingBase / years m1TotalReplaced = annualSafeWD × yearsToRetirement m1SurvivorGap = max(0, projectedNetIncomeTotal - m1TotalReplaced)

Level annual income drawn from funding base over the full horizon, assuming full depletion at retirement.

Module 2 — Max Withdrawal (Aggressive)

// Annual cycle: balance = balance × (1 + maxROI) covered = min(balance, projectedNetIncome) balance = max(0, balance - covered)

Fund grows at ROI, then survivor draws full need. Continues until exhaustion. Fully covered years are green; shortfalls are red.

Present Value of Level Gap

annualGap = survivorGap ÷ yearsToRetirement DB_Needed = annualGap × (1 - (1+roi)^(-years)) / roi If roi = 0: DB_Needed = survivorGap

Converts cumulative survivor gap to equivalent level annual gap, then discounts to present-value lump sum needed today.

2. Disability Insurance Module

2.1 Core Gap Calculator

LTD Monthly Gross & Net

ltdMonthlyGross = min(annualIncome × coveragePercent ÷ 12, monthlyCap) ltdNetMonthly = ltdMonthlyGross × 0.70 (if taxable) ltdNetMonthly = ltdMonthlyGross (if non-taxable)

Applies coverage percentage, caps benefit, and applies simplified 30% tax haircut if applicable.

Income Loss & Projections

totalNetMonthly = ltdNetMonthly + privateDiMonthly incomeLossNet = (annualIncome × 0.70 ÷ 12) - totalNetMonthly annualIncomeNetAtAge[year] = annualIncome × (1+growthRate)^year × 0.70

Immediate monthly shortfall vs combined benefits. Projects after-tax income forward for coverage comparison.

Annual Gap & Cumulative Metrics

annualGap[year] = max(0, netIncome - ltdAnnualBenefit - idIAnnualBenefit) totalGap = Σ annualGap[year] averageCoverageRate = totalCoverage ÷ totalProjectedIncome

Unprotected net income at each age. Cumulative gap shows maximum lifetime exposure. Average rate shows coverage efficiency.

2.2 Break-Even / Premium vs. Self-Insured

Investment Balance & Benefits

investmentBalance[m] = (balance[m-1] + premium) × (1 + monthlyRate) benefitsReceived = monthlyBenefit × min(monthsWithoutIncome, 80)

Simulates investing premiums in a side fund. Benefits capped at 80 months per lookup table boundary.

Break-Even Point

breakEvenMonths = log((premium + benefits × rate) / premium) / log(1 + rate) If rate = 0: breakEvenMonths = benefits ÷ premium

Months needed to self-insure before accumulated fund matches insurance benefit. Mirrors Excel NPER function.

2.3 Premium vs. Self-Insured — Retirement Projection

Future Value & Coverage Months

FV = monthlyPremium × ((1 + r/12)^n - 1) / (r/12) monthsOfCoverage = FV ÷ monthlyBenefit yearsOfCoverage = monthsOfCoverage ÷ 12

Standard future-value of an ordinary annuity. Shows equivalent disability months the invested premiums could fund at retirement.

2.4 Savings Bridge (Elimination Period)

Elimination Period & Runway

eliminationMonths = days ÷ 30.4375 savingsNeeded = monthlyExpenses × eliminationMonths savingsRunway = liquidSavings ÷ monthlyExpenses postBridgeRunway = savingsRemaining ÷ ongoingMonthlyGap

Models liquid savings depletion during waiting period. Calculates post-LTD monthly gap and remaining runway.

2.5 SSDI Benefit Estimator

PIA Calculation (2025 Bend Points)

AIME ≈ careerAvgAnnual ÷ 12 Tier1 = 0.90 × min(AIME, 1226) Tier2 = 0.32 × max(0, min(AIME, 7391) - 1226) Tier3 = 0.15 × max(0, AIME - 7391) PIA = round(Tier1 + Tier2 + Tier3, $0.10) Monthly SSDI = min(PIA, 3822)

Implements SSA formula using 2025 brackets. Applies statutory maximum cap of $3,822/mo.

Replacement Rate & Waiting Period

replacementRateGross = Monthly SSDI ÷ (annualIncome ÷ 12) monthlyGap = max(0, monthlyIncome - Monthly SSDI) waitingLoss = (annualIncome ÷ 12) × 5

Calculates income gap not covered by SSDI and income lost during mandatory 5-month waiting period.

2.6 LTD Benefit Taxation Calculator

Tax Computation & Replacement Rates

federalTax = piecewise bracket tax on (grossBenefit - stdDeduction) stateTax = taxableBenefit × stateRate netAnnualBenefit = annualGrossBenefit - totalTax netReplacementRate = netAnnualBenefit ÷ preTaxAnnualIncome

Calculates actual after-tax take-home pay during disability. Compares net LTD to net pre-disability income for true replacement rate.

2.7 Job Comparison Module

Job A vs Job B Protection

// Job A (No IDI): groupCovered_A = min(groupLTD_A, salary_A) gap_A = salary_A - groupCovered_A // Job B (With IDI): effectiveSalary_B = salary_A - premium_B groupCovered_B = min(groupLTD_B, effectiveSalary_B) idiCovered_B = min(IDI_B, max(0, effectiveSalary_B - groupCovered_B - premium_B)) gap_B = max(0, effectiveSalary_B - groupCovered_B - idiCovered_B) advantage = (groupCovered_B + idiCovered_B) - groupCovered_A

Models real take-home impact after IDI premium. Shows additional annual disability income available in Job B vs A.

3. Liability Module

Wage Garnishment Risk

incomeRisk = Σ annualIncome × (1+growth)^year × garnishmentRate householdWageRisk = primaryIncomeRisk + spouseIncomeRisk

Cumulative income at risk of garnishment over remaining career using default 25% maximum legal limit.

Assets at Risk & Total Liability

nonQualifiedRisk = max(inputs.nonQualified, investments + savings + business) totalHouseholdLiability = householdWageRisk + nonQualifiedRisk totalCoverage = autoLiability + umbrella

Excludes qualified retirement accounts. Sums wage exposure and non-protected assets to find total exposure.

Gap & Recommendation Logic

liabilityGap = max(0, totalLiability - totalCoverage) recommendedUmbrella = max(netWorthAtRisk, income×5, 1000000) shortfall = max(0, recommendedUmbrella - currentUmbrella)

Gap shows unprotected exposure. Recommended umbrella uses multi-factor approach: net worth, 5x income, or $1M minimum.

4. Unemployment Module

Cash Flow & Reserves

monthlyCashFlow = monthlyIncome - burnRate minReserve = burnRate × 3 optimalReserve = burnRate × 6 dangerThreshold = burnRate × 1.5 runwayRaw = emergencySavings ÷ burnRate

Baseline surplus/deficit. Standard benchmarks for emergency fund adequacy. Savings-only runway calculation.

Offsets & Effective Runway

monthlyOffset = severance + unemploymentBenefit netBurn = burnRate - monthlyOffset totalOffsetPool = (severance × duration) + (benefit × duration) effectiveRunway = (savings + totalOffsetPool) ÷ burnRate

Combines severance and state benefits. Calculates theoretical upper-bound runway when all resources are pooled.

Month-by-Month Simulation

required[m] = max(0, burnRate - offsetIncome[m]) covered[m] = min(reserveBalance, required[m]) shortfall[m] = max(0, required[m] - reserveBalance) reserveBalance[m] = max(0, reserveBalance[m-1] - required[m])

Granular monthly tracking of offset income, savings drawdown, and depletion timeline.
